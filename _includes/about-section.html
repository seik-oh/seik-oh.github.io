Situated at the intersection of Computer Science, HCI, and Learning Science, I am interested in how principles from cognitive psychology and accessibility can inform the design of technologies that make learning more intuitive, equitable, and sustainable.

In my work, I combine qualitative methods (interviews, usability studies) with quantitative analysis (behavioral metrics, error rates) to generate actionable insights. My goal is to bridge research and engineering, moving from research → prototype → insight-backed decisions that create inclusive and measurable improvements in learning technologies.
